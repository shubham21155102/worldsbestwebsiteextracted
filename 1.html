My Process

Daniel Spatzek Project start to finish 6 Steps Version .02
1

Briefing

Send me a detailed brief with the following information: who you are, core values, business goals, target audience, what you sell, required features, scope of the project (or sitemap), timeframes and deadlines, examples of other websites you like and everything else you think is important.

2

Evaluation

I will take a close look at your brief and assess whether the project meets the terms and requirements of both parties. If there is a match, we will have a online meeting to discuss how to proceed.

3

KickOff

We discuss approaches and concepts to find the perfect solution for your needs. Multiple sessions can be held if necessary.

4

Design

This is where the magic happens. I will work on art direction, concept and design. In the first phase I present a mood board or 2-3 screens showing the look and feel of the final product. After the direction has been approved, I design the entire project.

5

Development

After approval of the design/mood, the respective screen design is developed. I will update regularly on the status of development so you always have a clear insight into the progress.

6

Quality Assurance

After development is complete, we continue to test on multiple browsers and devices to fix bugs. After your final approval, the site is ready to ship.

FAQ

01. Daniel, how much is a website?
(asked by clients)

Hmm, let me ask you something first: How much is a car? Depends on which one you want, right? Same with websites: Costs depend on your requirements, scope and features. Therefore your budget defines what I can do for you. But don't worry, I am happy to tell you exactly what you will get for your budget once it is defined.

02. Daniel, how long does it take to make such awesome websites?
(asked by clients)

Depends on requirements and scope, but typically smaller projects take ~2 months and larger ones 3-4 months (from kickoff to go-live).

03. Daniel, can you also deal with bigger projects ?
(asked by clients)

Sure! I own a small remote agency (Spatzek Studio) which is part of an international network of design & development superstars.

04. Daniel, are you a designer or developer?
(asked by clients)

I am both. All my projects have been designed from scratch by myself. Many of my websites were one man projects built with Nuxt.js + Storyblok CMS. For larger projects I work with my remote core team from Spatzek Studio.

05. Daniel, what web design trends can we expect to see in the near future?
(asked by designers)

Old TVs on the streets will be the next big thing, according to trusted sources. Wallpaper patterns behind FAQs are also pretty hot right now. And rumor has it that every website needs at least one image of a guy standing on a volcano. Also keep an eye on the 'draw crap on fine-art movement'.
